# Interview Guide

The user wants help remembering. Ask questions before drafting and let the answers be messy.

## Initial Question Batch

Ask 6-9 questions. Keep them concrete and easy to answer in bullets.

Use or adapt:

1. What month is this for, and what `lastUpdated` date should the page use?
2. If this month had one headline or emotional center, what would it be?
3. What consumed most of your work energy? Include projects, company changes, milestones, conflicts, things shipped, or things that changed direction.
4. What changed in your life outside work: family, home, health, routines, travel, hobbies, or relationships?
5. Which side projects mattered this month? For each, say whether it advanced, paused, died, restarted, or crossed an important line.
6. What felt hard, unresolved, disappointing, anxious, or surprisingly good?
7. What specific dates, names, releases, links, products, places, objects, metrics, or small details should not be missed?
8. What did you start, stop, kill, pause, or decide to go all-in on?
9. Looking ahead, what are the next few things you expect to focus on?

If recent pages mention known recurring threads, personalize the questions with them: Alan, openrouter-rs, DreamNum/Univer, Workus/Marco, Kira, Sealbox, Founders Fight Club, cycling, music, home, cat, or other current threads found in `src/content/now/`.

## Follow-Up Probes

Use follow-ups when the first answers are too thin or too event-only:

- What changed your mind this month?
- What would future you want to remember about this period?
- Which part are you avoiding because it is uncomfortable to write?
- Did anything cross from "experiment" to "real"?
- Did anything stop making sense?
- What was the smallest concrete detail that captures the month?
- Was there a private feeling behind the public milestone?
- What is still not solved?

## Synthesis Checklist

Before drafting, make sure there is enough material for:

- One clear month-level arc.
- At least one work/technical section.
- At least one personal/life/emotional section, unless the user says the month was purely work-focused.
- One section about side projects or creative work, if any existed.
- A closing direction for the next month.

If any required piece is missing, ask 1-3 targeted follow-up questions instead of guessing.

## Drafting From Chinese Notes

If the user answers in Chinese, preserve the meaning but draft in natural English. Do not translate literally when English idiom would sound stiff. Keep names, technical terms, and dates exact.
