# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site serves a mixed audience: people evaluating Morris Liu for collaboration or hiring,
technical peers interested in Rust systems and agent-runtime work, and readers or users following
the open-source projects behind that work. They usually arrive through a link, search result, post,
or GitHub profile and need to quickly understand what Morris builds, how he thinks, and where to go
next.

## Product Purpose

realmorrisliu.com is Morris Liu's personal website and long-form technical home. It exists to make
his systems, agent infrastructure, writing, professional history, current focus, and personal
creative interests legible in one place. Success means visitors can form a precise impression of his
engineering taste, navigate naturally to writing or resume material, and leave with enough context
to follow, collaborate, hire, or reach out.

## Brand Personality

Precise, letter-like, systems-minded. The site should feel like a carefully written note from a
technical builder: restrained, direct, thoughtful, and materially grounded in real work rather than
performative portfolio effects.

## Anti-references

Do not turn the site into a SaaS landing page with hero metrics, repeated feature-card grids,
gradient text, all-purpose AI/productivity aesthetics, or conversion-heavy calls to action. Do not
make it a maximalist portfolio that competes with the writing. Avoid generic editorial affectation
for its own sake, repeated tiny uppercase section labels, decorative glassmorphism, and visual
systems that make the site look template-generated.

## Design Principles

1. Write like a person, structure like an engineer.
2. Let real artifacts carry credibility: posts, projects, resume entries, current work, and moments.
3. Keep navigation embedded in the reading flow instead of imposing a marketing shell.
4. Use restraint deliberately, with enough hierarchy and rhythm that pages do not feel unfinished.
5. Preserve bilingual and long-form readability over decorative density.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for text contrast and interaction clarity. Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep
content readable without JavaScript-heavy interactions, and preserve strong typography for both
English and Chinese content. Links, dates, tags, language switching, and resume navigation should
remain clear to keyboard and screen-reader users.
